Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Innovation is the driving force behind Bangladesh 2.0, creating a future where new ideas and technology pave the way for progress. By supporting startups, encouraging creativity, and embracing digital tools, it envisions a Bangladesh ready to solve challenges and seize opportunities on the global stage.

Sustainability

Sustainability is at the heart of this vision, ensuring that development doesn’t come at the cost of the environment. From adopting renewable energy to championing eco-friendly practices, Bangladesh 2.0 is about building a greener future for generations to come.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure is the backbone of Bangladesh 2.0, focusing on building better roads, smarter cities, and stronger connections. With modern transportation, reliable energy, and faster communication networks, it’s creating a foundation for a more efficient and connected nation.

Empowerment

Empowerment is about giving everyone a fair chance to succeed. Bangladesh 2.0 is committed to improving education, promoting equality, and creating opportunities for all—so that every citizen, regardless of background, can contribute to and benefit from the country's progress.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation's trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness

“When we want to help the poor, we usually offer them charity. Most often we use charity to avoid recognizing the problem and finding the solution for it. Charity becomes a way to shrug off our responsibility. But charity is no solution to poverty. Charity only perpetuates poverty by taking the initiative away from the poor. Charity allows us to go ahead with our own lives without worrying about the lives of the poor. Charity appeases our consciences.”

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
